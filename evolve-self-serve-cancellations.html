Guided Self-Serve Cancellations Designed to Reduce Cost

Overview

Evolve is a vacation rental management company focused on delivering 5‚Äëstar guest experiences and management services for vacation rental owners. Most of Evolve‚Äôs owner users fall within the 45‚Äì64 age range and are distributed across the US.

Problem

In the vacation rental industry, homeowners often need the ability to cancel guest bookings quickly, whether due to maintenance issues, double bookings, or unexpected personal circumstances. At Evolve, however, the process was manual and cumbersome: owners had to submit a support request, call, or email to make a cancellation. Because cancellations are time-sensitive, this dependency on frontline teams frustrated owners and left guests with less time to adjust their travel plans. The lack of a self-serve option not only slowed resolution but also eroded trust in the platform‚Äôs ability to support both owners and guests in critical moments.

Solution

I designed a self-serve cancellation flow for the responsive web Owner Account experience. Since booking cancellation rules differ across distribution partners like Airbnb and Vrbo, the solution needed to balance clarity, guidance, and compliance. The final design combined step-by-step instructions, education, and document collection to simplify what had been a complex approval process. At the same time, I built in strategic content to help owners understand the impact cancellations have on their rental performance‚Äîencouraging fewer cancellations overall.

Impact

The new self-serve booking cancellation tool saw immediate adoption, with ~20% of the cancellations completed by owners in the first month. Our data dashboard revealed significant drop-off rates in each successive step or section. Drop-off was good in this case, signaling that owners were pausing to reconsider canceling after understanding the consequences. In other words, the tool not only enabled self-service but also successfully influenced behavior, helping reduce unnecessary cancellations.

The problem, in detail

Guest booking cancellations were inherently complex. Since Evolve owners‚Äô properties were listed across multiple distribution partners like Airbnb and Vrbo, each with its own rules and penalties, owners faced varying requirements when canceling. Cancellation fees acted as a deterrent, discouraging owners from backing out of bookings since cancellations negatively impacted their performance, search ranking, and long-term guest satisfaction. At the same time, these penalties upheld trust and credibility on the platform by holding owners to consistent standards.

Fees were calculated based on how close the cancellation was to the guest‚Äôs check-in date and the reason for canceling, often amounting to a percentage of the total booking. In certain cases, owners could request a waive fee, but this required timely submission of specific documentation‚Äîadding another layer of complexity to the process.

Speed to cancel a booking was a clear issue. Due to the complex process of varying distribution partners, users couldn't handle their own cancellation without additional education. Lastly, users didn't understand or believe there were short- and long-term impacts on their property performance caused by canceling. There were 3 main themes: speed, education, and persuasion.

  • Speed: How could I design a new self-serve tool that simplifies a once complex process and makes canceling faster and easier than the original request or call methods?
  • Education: How could I leverage the user's selected cancellation reason to counter with a highly personalized, alternative solution that makes them pause and reconsider their decision?
  • Persuasion: How could I design the process so users are aware of and acknowledges the permanent loss of value so they're fully informed before finalizing the cancellation?

owner feedback

Users felt like they should be able to cancel guest bookings on their own. They were appreciative that frontline teams handled rebooking guests but were frustrated it took longer than expected.

Digging into the cancellation reasons for one year

Discovery

I took a step back to understand the current state of the cancellation process through the lens of speed, education, and persuasion. How might we be hitting or missing the mark from the user and internal team perspectives?

To better understand the current process and its impact on the speed:

  • Collaborated with frontline team to update fee process. I reviewed all the cancellation reasons, grouped them into clearer categories, and updated outdated options to improve user comprehension and decrease time to selection.

To understand where we may have had gaps in education:

  • Audited partner cancellation rules with a goal of simplifying the process: I documented the different cancellation rules and requirements from partner platforms like Airbnb and Vrbo. This helped define my content strategy for the tool and how I could simplify the instructions to best fit a majority of the rules.
  • Communication & resources audit: I audited the communications and content owners received around cancellations (i.e. emails, help center articles), then simplified them with Marketing to make the process easier to follow.

To assess where we could have better informed or persuaded users to reconsider their decision:

  • Robust flow analysis: I analyzed the current manual flow of requesting to finalizing a cancellation. What information were we communicating to the users to help them reconsider their decision? What was missing?

Toward the end, I went through several iterations of wireframes, discussing variations with my PM and engineer, and refining based on the usability tests that were conducted with users. I tested each variation to see each impact on speed, education, and persuasion.

Testing

I created a prototype, designing a hybrid solution based on the 3 variations of wireframes.

Overall, the cancellation flow performed well with users saying it was fast and easy to use. All users successfully navigated the process and clearly understood that their request was in progress. They also consistently valued the alternative options, with most indicating a preference for offering a discount instead of canceling. Based on the feedback, it seemed like the solution had a considerable impact on the user's perceived speed of the process and comprehension of the cancellation outcome. I decided to move forward with Version 2 for final designs.

Solution

Based on the test findings, I designed a solution that focused on delivering personalized, high-value alternative options to create for the user a moment of reflection (e.g. providing guest discounts). I sprinkled education throughout the process by way of tips and links to external resources with the goal of communicating the potential negative impact of canceling. I also simplified the whole process by trimming down the amount of text on screen and creating an all-in-one cancellation tool for canceling and requesting a waiver to minimize back and forth seen in the current process.

The final solution had a total of 6 steps. This was intentional as I wanted to make sure we had room to education and inform the user before they made their final decision. You can dive into the figma for the nitty gritty details.
